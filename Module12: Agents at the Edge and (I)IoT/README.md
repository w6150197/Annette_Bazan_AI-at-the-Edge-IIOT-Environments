This module introduces the concept of AI agents and their role in shaping modern intelligent systems across both digital and physical environments. It explains how AI agents operate autonomously by perceiving their environment, making decisions, and learning from feedback through reinforcement learning. The module explores real-world applications such as virtual assistants, recommendation systems, self-driving vehicles, robotics, and smart home technologies. It also highlights emerging advancements in generative AI, brain-computer interfaces (BCIs), and human-computer interaction, emphasizing how AI agents are evolving toward more adaptive, creative, and personalized systems. Finally, the module encourages critical thinking about the ethical and societal implications of predictive and autonomous AI systems.
